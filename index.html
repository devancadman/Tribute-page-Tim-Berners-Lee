Tim-Berners Lee


Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee is an English computer scientist who invented the World Wide Web. Berners-Lee enabled a system to be able to view web pages (hypertext documents) through the internet. He also serves as a director for the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) which overseas standards for the internet and World Wide Web. Berners-Lee has also been concerned about issues relating to freedom of information and censorship on the internet.

Tim-Berners Lee Timeline

  • JUN 8, 1955

    1955 Tim-Berners Lee

    Born in London England. To parents Conway Berners Lee and Mary Lee Woods.

  • AUG 21, 1969

    Schooling

    Went to Emanual School London from 1969 to 1973

  • AUG 21, 1973

    Graduation College

    1973 to 1976 graduated at the Queens College, Oxford university England where he recieved 1st class degree in physics

  • AUG 21, 1978

    Early work

    1978, Tim worked at D.G. Nash Limited where he wrote typesetting software.

  • JUN 21, 1980

    First Job

    Tim got a contract job as a software consultant in a company called CERN. He created the idea of sharing ideas quickly by a system called Enquire (this is similar to the world wide web).

  • AUG 21, 1981

    New Job

    Started in a new job called John Pooles image computer systems. This gave him experience in computer networking.

  • AUG 21, 1984

    Return to CERN

    He went back to work at CERN with a permanent persition.

  • AUG 21, 1989

    Internet oppurtunity

    Tim saw that he could join a company called CERN which is the largest internet node in Europe.

  • AUG 21, 1989

    Changing original idea

    Tim Berners Lee and Robert Cailliau made changes to his original thoughts and ideas and created a software that enabled anyone to get information from the computer they were using this is the design of the World Wide Web. This was the first browser.

  • Aug 6, 1991

    created the first web browser and editor

    “Info.cern.ch” was the address of the world’s first-ever web site and web server, running on a NeXT computer at CERN. This was launched on 6 August 1991. It explained the World Wide Web concept and gave users an introduction to getting started with their own websites.

  • AUG 21, 1992

    50 web servers

    In 1992 there were over 50 web servers in the world.

  • AUG 21, 1993

    Hits by 1993

    By summer 1993 the first website was getting 10 thousand hits a day.

  • AUG 21, 1994

    World Wide Web (W3C)

    In 1994, Berners-Lee founded W3C (World Wide Web consortium) at the Laboratory of Computer Science (LCS) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Boston. This is an organisation to try to improve the quality and standard of the world wide web.

  • 2004

    He was knighted by H.M. Queen Elizabeth

    “For services to the global development of the Internet”
    He has received over 10 honorary doctorates, is a member of the Internet Hall of Fame, and was awarded the Finland Millennium Prize in 2004.

  • 2007

    Berners-Lee was awarded the UK’s Order of Merit

    – A personal gift of the monarch limited to just 24 living recipients.

  • 2009

    Web Science Trust & World Wide Web Foundation

    He is a founding Director of the Web Science Trust (WST) launched in 2009 to promote research and education in Web Science, the multidisciplinary study of humanity connected by technology. Berners-Lee is also a Director of the World Wide Web Foundation, launched in 2009 to coordinate efforts to further the potential of the Web to benefit humanity.
